About · 03

A dissertation, a device, an app.

Commuta is an MSc Connected Environments dissertation project at the Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis (CASA), University College London. It brings together hardware, firmware, and a mobile app around a single research question: what are Underground commuters actually breathing?

01 / Aim

The research question.

Fixed monitoring stations tell us about outdoor air, but they don't follow anyone through a station, along a platform, or onto a carriage. Personal exposure — what a specific person actually breathes across a specific journey — is much harder to measure, and much less well understood.

Commuta is an attempt to close that gap for London Underground commuters. The project examines how station-level dwell time and line type influence a passenger's personal exposure to particulate matter, and whether a wearable device can produce a usable personal picture of that exposure.

The device measures continuously as the wearer travels. The companion app turns that data into something legible: what you breathed, where, and how it compares to the day before.
