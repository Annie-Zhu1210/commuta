What's really
in the air down there?

Commuta is a portable air quality monitor for London Underground commuters. It clips onto a bag, measures the air around you as you travel, and turns those readings into a picture of what you're actually breathing on your daily commute.

01 / The problem

The air on the Underground is not the air above it.

Deep-level Tube platforms are poorly ventilated. Brake dust, wheel wear, and rail grinding release fine metallic particulates that build up in the air, and commuters can be exposed to levels many times higher than at street level for significant stretches of every working day.

Fixed monitoring stations tell us about outdoor air, but they don't follow you underground, through a platform, and onto a carriage. Personal exposure — what a specific person actually breathes across a specific journey — is much harder to measure. Commuta is a way to measure it.

Deployment Personal & portable Worn on a bag; battery-powered; discreet enough for daily commuting use.
Scale One person, one journey Measures what you breathe, where you are, minute by minute.
02 / What it measures

Five signals, one small device.

A single palm-sized unit reads the air across five channels and two ambient conditions, sampling continuously as you move.

PM2.5 / PM10 Particulate matter Fine and coarse dust. The signature pollutant of Tube platforms.
CO₂ Carbon dioxide A proxy for how well a space is ventilated.
VOC Volatile organic compounds Chemical emissions from materials, cleaning products, and people.
NOx Nitrogen oxides Traffic-related pollutants relevant to street-level portions of a commute.
Temperature Ambient °C Context for the platform environment.
Humidity & pressure RH · hPa Compensation data for sensor accuracy and altitude reference.
03 / How it works

A device, a phone, a picture of your air.

01

The device measures

A palm-sized unit with five air-quality sensors, a rechargeable battery, and a small button interface. Clips to your bag and starts reading as soon as you step out.

02

It talks to your phone

Readings stream to the Commuta companion app over Bluetooth Low Energy. If you're out of range, the device buffers locally and syncs when you reconnect.

03

You see your air

The app shows what you're breathing right now, what you breathed on past journeys, and where along your commute the air was worst.
